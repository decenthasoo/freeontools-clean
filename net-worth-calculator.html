FAQs About the Net Worth Calculator

What is net worth?

Net worth is the difference between what you own (assets) and what you owe (liabilities). If you have more assets than liabilities, you have a positive net worth. If your liabilities are greater than your assets, you have a negative net worth.

Should I include everything I own?

Yes! Include everything of value—savings, property, investments, even things like cars or collectibles. Every asset adds up.

Do I include all of my debts?

Yep, include all debts—credit cards, student loans, mortgages, car loans, etc. All liabilities should be counted to get an accurate picture of your financial health.

How accurate is this calculator?

It’s as accurate as the numbers you put into it! If you accurately enter your assets and liabilities, you’ll get an accurate calculation of your net worth.
