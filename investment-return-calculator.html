FAQs About the Investment Return Calculator

What does "return on investment" (ROI) mean?

Return on investment (ROI) is a measure of the profitability of an investment. It shows how much money you've made (or lost) relative to your original investment. The higher the ROI, the better the investment performance.

How is the total return different from the annualized return?

Total return shows the overall gain or loss on an investment, including any dividends or interest earned.

Annualized return gives you an average annual return over the time period of the investment, allowing you to compare it to other investments over the same period.

Can I use the Investment Return Calculator for multiple investments?

Yes! You can use it to track multiple investments, either one at a time or by comparing them over the same time frame.

Can I adjust for inflation in the Investment Return Calculator?

Some calculators allow you to adjust for inflation in your investment return calculations. It’s a great way to see if your investment has truly kept pace with the cost of living. Look for that option in advanced calculators or adjust manually.
