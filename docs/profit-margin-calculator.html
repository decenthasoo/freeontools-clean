FAQs About the Profit Margin Calculator

What is profit margin?

Profit margin is the percentage of sales revenue that exceeds the costs of producing and selling your product or service. A higher margin means you’re making more profit for every dollar of revenue.

How do I calculate profit margin?

To calculate gross profit margin, subtract your cost of goods sold (COGS) from your revenue, then divide by revenue and multiply by 100. To calculate net profit margin, subtract all your expenses from revenue, divide by revenue, and multiply by 100.

What’s the difference between gross and net profit margin?

Gross profit margin looks at how much you make after covering the cost of producing your product. Net profit margin looks at how much you make after covering all expenses, including operating costs, taxes, and interest.

Can this calculator help with pricing?

Absolutely! By calculating your profit margins, you can adjust your pricing to ensure you’re making enough profit to cover all your costs and make a sustainable profit.
