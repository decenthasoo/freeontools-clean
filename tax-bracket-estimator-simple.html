FAQs About the Tax Bracket Estimator

What is a tax bracket?

A tax bracket is a range of income that is taxed at a particular rate. The more you earn, the higher your tax bracket and the greater the percentage of your income you pay in taxes.

How do tax brackets work?

In a progressive tax system, your income is taxed in chunks. For example, if you’re in the 25% tax bracket, only the portion of your income that falls within that range is taxed at 25%. Income below that level is taxed at lower rates.

Can I use this tool to estimate my tax refund?

No, the Tax Bracket Estimator focuses only on your tax liability (how much you owe based on your income). It doesn’t account for other factors like deductions, credits, or taxes already withheld.

Do I need to know all my income details?

Yes, to get an accurate estimate, you’ll need to know your total gross income. But don’t worry about things like deductions or other tax credits—this tool is just to give you a basic idea of where you fall.
